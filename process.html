The Process Of Game Making

The process of making video games is mainly split into 7 parts. Planning, Pre-Production, Production,
Testing, Pre-Launch, Launch, Post-Launch. There are a lot of things that developers must ask themselves
and think about when it comes to game development. A lot of these are what-if questions and soley depend
on how the game is recieved by the public and how well the game will sell. Here are some of the things that
happen and some of the questions asked in each stage of development.

Planning Pre-Production Production Testing Pre-Launch Launch Post-Launch
The idea has to be solid. Deciding on a stroy narrative. Characters are made,rendered, and animated. Where are our mistakes? Marketing and advertisement begins. Are there any last minute bugs to fix? How is the public recieving the game?
Who is the game made for? What kind of mechanics do we want? Developers start coding the game itself Are all assets in the right place? Prepare a Beta copy for the public to try. How much are we expecting to earn on release? What new features can we add?
How much will it cost to make? Deciding on visuals and character designs. Environments are crafted and come to life. Is this boring to our targeted audience? Is our gameplay good enough to show to the public? What do we do if the game flops? What can we do to keep players excited about the game?
